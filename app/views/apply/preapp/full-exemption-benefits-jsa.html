Based on what you've told us You get help with NHS costs and don't need to apply

Because you get Income-based Jobseeker's Allowance:

You get free:

  • NHS prescriptions
  • NHS dental check-ups and treatment
  • sight tests
  • NHS wigs and fabric supports

You get money off:

  • new glasses or contact lenses
  • repairing or replacing your glasses or contact lenses
  • travel for NHS treatment

    If you were referred for this treatment by a GP, hospital doctor, dentist or optician


Any children under 20 included on your Income-based Jobseeker's Allowance claim are also entitled to the same help.

How to get your help

In most cases, you don't have to do anything more, although you may have to show proof you get Income-based Jobseeker's Allowance to your pharmacist, optician or dentist.

You can use your latest benefit letter or award notice as proof.
If you have lost yours contact the Jobcentre Plus Helpline.

Help with travel for NHS treatment

Tell the hospital (or the place where you’re getting treatment) that you want to claim back the cost of your journey. They’ll tell you what you need to do.

You may need to show proof:

  • of your travel costs, for example, bus tickets
  • that you’re entitled to help

Warning You may be fined if you falsly declare you get Income-based Jobseeker's Allowance to get free NHS costs, so it is important that you know what benefit you get.
