Based on what you've told us You get help with NHS costs and don't need to apply

Because you get Child Tax Credit and have a household income of £15,276 or less:

You get free:

  • NHS prescriptions
  • NHS dental check-ups and treatment
  • sight tests
  • NHS wigs and fabric supports

You get money off:

  • new glasses or contact lenses
  • repairing or replacing your glasses or contact lenses
  • travel for NHS treatment

Your partner and any children under 20 included on your tax credit claim are also entitled to the same help.

How to get your help

You don't have to do anything more, although you may have to show your Tax Credit Exemption Certificate to your pharmacist, optician or dentist.

This is a small plastic card that will be sent to you within 6 weeks of you getting your tax credit award.

Lost and new cards

If you have lost your card, contact us to get a replacement. In the meantime use your tax credits award notice as proof.

If you haven't received a card, contact tax credits to check the household income they used to work out your tax credit payments.

Help with travel for NHS treatment

Tell the hospital (or the place where you’re getting treatment) that you want to claim back the cost of your journey. They’ll tell you what you need to do.

You may need to show proof:

  • of your travel costs, for example, bus tickets
  • that you’re entitled to help

Warning It's important you know what benefit you get - you can get a fine if you claim help with NHS costs and you're not entitled.
